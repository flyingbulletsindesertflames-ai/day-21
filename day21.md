Today was eye-opening.

We built a full Digital Footprint & Privacy Dashboard — and honestly, the results were more unsettling than expected.

Here's what we did 👇

🔍 Privacy Analysis
Mapped 15 daily-use apps (Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, Google Pay, PUBG, Spotify & more) to understand exactly how each one contributes to our online footprint. Turns out, 15 apps = 7 parent companies = one very large data trail.

⚠️ Risk Assessment
Scored our exposure across location, financial behaviour, social graph, biometrics & identity. Our Digital Footprint Score came in at 74/100 — Significant. Privacy Score? 38/100 — Fair at best.

🧠 Digital Twin Modeling
This was the most fascinating part. We estimated what platforms could infer about us — age range, spending habits, daily routines, music taste, social patterns — without ever asking directly. Algorithms don't need answers when they have behaviour.

🛡️ Actionable Security
We didn't just analyse the problem — we built a Privacy Improvement Simulator. Revoke location access. Delete TikTok. Enable Google auto-delete. Switch to DuckDuckGo. Each action translates to a measurable score improvement.

The biggest lesson?
You don't have to be hacked to be exposed. Every tap, search, and payment is a data point — and someone is always collecting.

Silent data is still data.
